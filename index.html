Correspondence from Vellynne Harpell

Tenth Black Staff of Blackstaff Academy

1489 DR, Hammer (Second Tenday)
To: Rothbart
Delivered by snowy owl familiar to the Northlook Inn

Dear Rothbart,

I trust this missive finds you well-rested after your eventful journey from East Haven. Word travels quickly through Bryn Shander, and your party's heroic escort of seven hundred refugees has not gone unnoticed. The town buzzes with tales of your group's valor against the vampire-yeti assault.

Following our meeting in Bryn Shander, I took the liberty of returning to the battlefield to retrieve the vampire brides' remains for study. Such specimens rarely present themselves for examination, and I suspect their condition may reveal crucial intelligence about the undead threat we face. I will keep you apprised of any findings that emerge from my research. How curious that a being touched by undeath should prove so effective at thwarting vampiric predators—your divine powers clearly burned particularly bright against those pale-skinned seductresses.

The magical theorist in me is absolutely fascinated by your acquisition of those Netherese artifacts from Targos. The stones you carry represent some of the most sophisticated enchantment work from the fallen empire—devices capable of anchoring tremendous magical energies to specific locations. Your visit to Black Iron Blades was quite the spectacle, I'm told. The transformation of that red dragon heart into those rather impressive bracers must have been remarkable to witness.

I would be most interested to hear your firsthand account of the audience with Speaker Duvessa, particularly regarding the political maneuvering you witnessed. My sources suggest Naerth Maxildannar's presence complicated matters considerably. Did your enhanced senses detect anything unusual about the Targos delegation? Such intelligence could prove valuable as we navigate the increasingly treacherous political landscape of the Ten Towns.

Your party's newfound wealth— ten thousand gold in diamonds, I hear—positions you perfectly for the challenges ahead. Though I suspect you view my research with distaste, the practical applications of necromantic knowledge may prove essential in dealing with both the undead threat at Reghed Glacier and our upcoming rendezvous with your Illithid acquaintance. Sometimes the most distasteful tools prove the most effective.

I look forward to our meeting at the Northlook, where we can discuss strategy for locating Dzaan's spire and accessing the Nautiloid. The threads of fate seem to be drawing together—vampire masters, ancient cities, and aberrant allies. Quite the tapestry we're weaving, wouldn't you agree?

With anticipation of our collaboration,

Vellynne Harpell

Tenth Black Staff of Blackstaff Academy

P.S. I've heard remarkable things about a new three-armed fighting technique. Such adaptability in combat suggests you're becoming more comfortable with unconventional solutions—a promising development for what lies ahead.
